How recommendations work
Pareto frontier: a model is kept iff no other candidate has both higher quality AND lower price. Frontier is then sorted by quality descending, so the top row is the best achievable quality under the filters.

Best value (knee point): among frontier models with quality ≥ 50% of the top, pick the one that maximises quality / price. Shown separately only when it's not the top-quality model — i.e., when there's a real trade-off to make.

Effective price (Eff$ column) accounts for capability fallback: for simple/default/complex, non-vision candidates are charged 0.9 × prompt + 0.1 × multimodal_slot.prompt because image messages route to the multimodal slot. Assumes 10% of messages contain images. Vision-capable models pay nominal price.

Quality axis is role-specific: simple/default/complex → AA Agentic Index; multimodal/compaction → AA Intelligence Index; classifier → 1/TTFT when speed data available, else Intelligence.
